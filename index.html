About Me

I am a first-year Ph.D. student at Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Professor Bryan Parno. I am broadly interested in formal methods and programming languages. My current project is on Rust verification.

In the past, I have worked on automating verification of collision avoidance, verifying implementations of distributed systems protocols, and extending CompCert guarantees to concurrent programs.

I received my bachelor's and master's degrees from the University of Michigan.
